About the Organization

We are an RSO at UC Berkeley geared towards providing graduate students with resources to explore careers in data science.


About the team

James Arnemann (Founder)     

I founded the graduate data science organization to address a need to provide resources for graduate students to start a career in data science. I am currently pursuing my PhD in Artificial Intelligence research, specifically Deep Learning and Machine Learning. In addition to my research, I have been involved with many outside Data Science projects and industry internships.

Sam Dixon     

I'm a PhD student in the Physics department at UC Berkeley, studying Type Ia supernovae as tools for measuring the expansion history of the Universe and investigating the nature of dark energy. My research makes use of many data science tools and techniques, but I try to expand my skill set with side projects and hackathon submissions too. I pull myself away from my computer to ride my bike and go to concerts around the Bay Area.

Katrina Kalantar     

I am a PhD Student in the UCB UCSF Joint Bioengineering Program, currently studying host response to infectious diseases using next-generation sequencing and bioinformatics approaches. I enjoy tackling challenging informatics projects and am passionate about applying large data analysis to improve human health. I am simultaneously excited by the possibilities enabled by application of similar analysis techcniques to day-to-day life. Outside of work, I enjoy playing ultimate frisbee and hiking.

Hyesoo Youn   

I am currently pursuing my PhD in Molecular and Cell Biology at UC Berkeley, with a concentration in Neurobiology. I thrive solving challenging problems and particularly enjoy experimental design and data analysis. Through a career as a data scientist, I aim to apply my knowledge and skill set to make the world a better place.
