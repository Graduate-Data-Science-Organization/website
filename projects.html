Predicting Properties of Structural Ceramics

Mentor: Chris Borg
Participants: Chris Bronner, Chih-Hao Hsu, Pra Bhattacharyya, Steve Drapcho

Building new products demands, without doubt, the ability to produce new materials with fine tuned properties. However, the conventional method of using theoretical and experimental results are slow and often serendipitous. Material informatics, a data driven approach to supplant the former techniques, is the use of experimental data on existing materials to predict new ones with suitable properties. Our team worked under the mentorship of Chris Borg, a Data Engineer at Citrine which is one of the first commercial platforms for material informatics. For our project, we worked with the NIST Structural Ceramics Dataset accessed through Citrination, an API developed by Citrine. Structural Ceramics are known for retaining their structural integrity under extreme conditions. We featurized our data set using Matminer, a python based tool to compute structural and compositional features of materials (e.g., packing efficiency of the crystal) from its chemical constitutions. Each member worked on using a machine learning model to capture the dependence of a single physical parameter on composition, crystallinity and temperature.

Our findings are summarized in https://github.com/ckborg/DSW2018
